💙💙💙Welcome to my persona for the station kiosk!💙💙💙

  • a. I selected the station kiosk as my interface.

  • b. Sketch of the station kiosk.

    • Description of my interface:

      The station kiosk is a machine on which people can buy tickets or print bought tickets and receipts.

  • c. Observations and interview summaries.

    • Observations:

      • Most of the users use the function scan.
      • They buy tickets online and scan for a paper ticket.
      • They are familiar with the functions.
      • They directly put the barcode under the scan camera.
      • A few users don't know where to start at first.

    • Interview summaries:

      • Most users are between 20 years old to 35 years old.
      • Most users use the machine to print the paper ticker and receipt.
      • People believe using kiosks are more convenient than going to the customer service desk.
      • People arrive around 10 minutes ahead to print tickets
      • People think kiosk is fast.

  • d. Empathy maps.

    • Empathy map for people seeking to print bought tickets:

      • Description of user:

        Mary has bought a ticket online and want to use the station kiosk to print the ticket the receipt.

      • Problem they met:

        • Can not clearly see where to start to print tickets.
        • Do not know where to scan the ticket code.
        • The print receipt choice is so small that she didn't notice and forget to print.

      • How and why the persona represents:

        This persona represents people who use the station kiosk to print a ticket and/or a receipt of the ticket they bought online. They are not very familiar with the kiosk. The persona represents them because it depicts their actions and thoughts when they are using the kiosk. The persona lists the problems they met and the questions they ask.

    • Empathy map for people seeking to buy tickets:

      • Description of user:

        David just decided to take the train so he comes to the station to buy the ticket. He doesn't want to interact with people or think the line to the travel agents is too line. So he comes to the station kiosk to buy a ticket.

      • Problem they met:

        • Cannot find the station he wants to go.
        • Not understanding the differences between tickets.
        • Did not find where to insert the card.

      • How and why the persona represents:

        This persona represents people who want to buy tickets at the station kiosk. It is representative because it summarizes the problems people met when they are buying tickets.

  • e. Storyboard.

    Storyboard 1.
    The weather is so great. Why not going to Boston to eat something delicious and take a shopping? David decides to go to the Amtrak station now!
    Storyboard 2.
    There are so many people in line. David doesn't want to waste time to buy a ticket through the travel agents. David decides to go to the kiosk to buy the ticket.
    Storyboard 3.
    It is the first time for David to use a kiosk. He is prompted to tap the "touch to begin" button to start buying.
    Storyboard 4.
    There are many different types of tickets provided. David needs to decide which type of ticket to choose. He wants to come back to providence at night so he chooses the round trip ticket.
    Storyboard 5.
    David is trying to find his destination station in the list. There are so many stations to choose. He wants to find the right one as soon as possible since there are people waiting for him in the line. Finally he finds it in a rush.
    Storyboard 6.
    There are still choices to make. David is not sure about the differences betweeen these types of seats. He asked the man behind him for suggestions. He chooses the cheapest one at last.
    Storyboard 7.
    David chooses to pay with a card. But he did not find the position to insert the card since it is his first time using the kiosk. He feels a little bit awkward looking for somewhere to insert his card in.
    Storyboard 8.
    After the payment is made, David gets his ticket from the kiosk successfully.
